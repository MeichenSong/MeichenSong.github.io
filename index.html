About Me

I am a Groseclose Postdoctoral Fellow at the H. Milton Stewart School of Industrial and Systems Engineering (ISyE), Georgia Institute of Technology, working with Professor Enlu Zhou. I received my Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics and Statistics from Stony Brook University in 2025 under the supervision of Professor Jiaqiao Hu.

My research focuses on developing simulation-based optimization algorithms for black-box stochastic systems under uncertainty, with the goal of quantifying and optimizing unknown risk functions. A central theme of my current work is Bayesian Risk Optimization (BRO) and its formulations to risk-averse Markov Decision Processes and reinforcement learning.
